Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Janulabdeen Abdul Kalam was the 11th president of India and also an Aerospace scientist. He was also known as "Missile man of India"
He served 5 years as a president in india from 2002 to 2007. He got India's highest civilian honour "Bharat Ratna" in 1997.
Kalam's life is an inspiration to all new generations because he was called Missile man. Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October in 1931 at Rameswaram in Tamil Nadu. Kalam was a hardworking student. He pursued his matriculation from a higher secondary school in Ramananthapuram.
In 1955 he graduated from Saint Joseph's College inTiruchirappalli in physics. He pursued his aerospace engineering from Madras Institute of Technology. Every Year 15th October is celebrated as World Student's Day. In 2010 the United Nations declared World Students Day to be celebrated on Dr Abdul Kalam's birthday to honour and mark his efforts towards promoting education.
Abdul's father's name was Jainulabdeen. His mother's name was Ashiamma. His mother was a housewife. His fathe was the owner of a boat. Kalam was the yougest of five siblings.
His favourite subject was mathematics from school days kalam was bright and hardworking studets. At the time of kalam's birth. His family was in a vary bad state. Because most of money and land lost by business. So from his early age onwards he helped his family.
After graduation, he became a member of the "Defence Research and Development organisation". After that he worked under the space scientiest at Vikram Sarabhai. In 1969 Kalam was transferred to Indian Space Research organisation (ISRO). The main project of kalam was Satellite Launch Vehical (SLV-III). He was the head of the project.
